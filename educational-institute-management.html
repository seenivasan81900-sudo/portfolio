Project Overview

MVP Product: Designed an Educational Institute Dashboard to manage student attendance and course levels. Focused on creating a smooth 3-panel connection between Admin, Staff, and Students for better tracking and communication.

Problem Statement

Students often forget to update their course progress through Google Forms, resulting in incomplete and inaccurate data . This creates confusion for managers, tutors, and the Managing Director, making it difficult to track learning progress and ensure students stay on schedule.

Design process

I used the Lean UX approach to design quickly and validate early. I began with user research and competitor insights, built a clear information architecture, and created low-fidelity wireframes to define the flow. After that, I moved to high-fidelity designs and refined them through feedback and iterations to improve the overall experience.

Research & Insights

To understand the real problems, I spoke with 1 manager from small institutions.

Here’s what I learned:

  • 1. Students frequently forget to update their progress
  • 2. Managers struggle to track real progress accurately
  • 3. No real-time visibility for tutors or students

From these findings, I focused on improving clarity, automation, and data visibility across the system.

Competitor Analysis

Before starting the design, I explored popular institute and school management systems to understand their strengths and pain points.

Most competitors focus on basic features like attendance and fee tracking, but they lack course level progress, student-staff performance insights, and a smooth fees collection flow.

Using ChatGPT during my research, I compared and analyzed existing systems to identify how my design could stand out.

Information Architecture

Organizing content and features into a clear structure that makes navigation simple and intuitive for users.

User Flow

To improve transparency and accuracy in student progress tracking, I designed a simple two-sided flow connecting Staff activity to Student visibility.

Staff Panel

  • Start Class
  • Select Language
  • Select Topics
  • Mark Completion Status → Update

Student Panel

  • View Course Progress
  • Tap Language Card (HTML)
  • View Topic List
  • See status for every topic
  • Track overall progress

Before (Current System)

  • Students manually fill Google Forms
  • Many forget or enter wrong details
  • Manager checks forms one by one
  • Progress reports are slow and inaccurate
  • No real-time view for tutors or students

After Solution (MVP Dashboard)

I created a system where staff starts the class, selects the language, chooses the topics, and updates progress during/after the session.

System Syncs Real-Time

  • Every time staff updates a topic, → students immediately see the updated status.
  • No Google Forms, no manual tracking, no forgetting.

Design Decision & Iteration Insight

Refining the design through continuous feedback and testing to enhance both usability and visual experience.

Improving Leaderboard Design

Initially, the design was visually strong but overly contrasted — bright colors and heavy decorations dominated the content. After review, I realized simplicity alone wasn't enough; the layout needed character and clarity..

Final Iteration

In the final iteration, I balanced minimalism with identity by using refined color tones, clear hierarchy, and subtle highlights to create a modern and clean interface. I introduced Gold, Silver, and Bronze performance stages to reflect real-world achievement levels, making student and staff performance easier to recognize at a glance. This approach keeps the focus on results while giving the dashboard a unique and professional personality.

Improving Student details page design

Old Version

  • Managers said: “Too many clicks to view full details.”
  • Student info and payment info were placed side-by-side with equal weight, making scanning slower.
  • Staff found it hard to cross-check data because the modal blocks the background.
  • No quick actions → everything hidden inside the modal.

After Design

New Version (Why It Works)

  • The student’s key data stays visible on the left side while the right side changes based on tabs (Attendance / Task / Fee).This reduces context switching.
  • Blue card = high-priority identity + payment details
  • Right section = dynamic module (attendance,tasks, fees)Makes scanning extremely fast for staff and managers.
  • Having everything in one screen reduces 2-3 step interactions.

Why Blue? - Color Psychology

Blue is used because it matches the brand's logo color and strongly supports the brand's identity. It communicates trust, reliability, and clarity—qualities that are essential for an education management system. Blue also helps users feel calm and focused, reducing cognitive load while navigating the dashboard. Its professional and stable tone makes it ideal for handling important student and staff information, building confidence that the system is dependable and easy to use.

Why Inter Font?

Inter was chosen because it is a modern, highly readable typeface designed specifically for digital interfaces. Its clean letterforms improve on-screen clarity, making information easy to scan—especially important for dashboards with data, tables, and performance metrics. Inter also supports a wide range of weights, allowing clear hierarchy and smooth visual flow across headings, labels, and body text. Overall, it keeps the design simple, professional, and accessible on all devices.
