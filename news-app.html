About Company

Namadha Vanigam Company has been working in the magazine and e-book services industry for the past six years. They are now looking to expand into digital products, specifically a mobile app. The company focuses on local news and market advertisements and aims to create a centralized platform for their offerings. They target native Tamil speakers, who are already users of other news apps. Their idea is to introduce a Native News app.

Project Brief

The primary goal was to design a modern, user-friendly app that lets users stay updated with daily news, save articles, receive notifications, and enable reporters to register â€” all while ensuring a smooth and engaging experience.

Design process

My design process is similar to that of my competitor Anaylse. I start by creating low-fidelity wireframes to outline user flows and structures. After that, I develop high-fidelity designs that focus on visual hierarchy and usability. Once I gather feedback, I iterate on the designs to improve both the user experience and the interface.

Target Users

The app is targeted at native Tamil-speaking users who:

  • Regularly consume news and ads via print or local WhatsApp groups
  • Already use popular news apps (like DailyHunt, Google News), but struggle to find district-level relevance
  • Prefer a clean, fast, Tamil-first experience

Competitor Analysis

I reviewed leading apps like Dailyhunt and Inshorts to understand how users browse, read, and save news. Most competitors focus on quick consumption but lack strong hyperlocal coverage and clear reporter contribution flows. These gaps guided my design to prioritize local news, easy bookmarking, and a simple reporter registration experience.

Iterative Design

1 Before

  • No search option, forcing users to scroll to find content.
  • No promotional or featured content block.
  • No save/bookmark option on article cards.
  • Text-over-image reduces readability (depends heavily on image color).

2 After

  • Added a prominent search bar at the top, allowing users to quickly find specific newsâ€”improves accessibility and reduces time-to-content.
  • Added a scrollable card carousel for ads or featured promotionsâ€”helping the app monetise and also highlight important campaigns.
  • Article cards include a star icon, giving users the ability to save important news for laterâ€”improves personalization and retention.

Reflection & Takeaways

Not every designer has the chance to create an end-to-end product. I value this opportunity, which has helped me grow as a product designer and impacted many people. Here are a few key takeaways.

Collaborating with a Developer

Collaboration is an essential quality for a product designer. I collaborated with managers and developers to deliver products to customers. This experience helped me achieve our goals as a team.

Accomplishing time-boxed goals

Setting goals and planning them within timelines was challenging. Although the project was time-boxed, we successfully delivered a minimum viable product that effectively served the users.
